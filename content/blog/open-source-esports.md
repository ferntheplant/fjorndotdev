Nobody _owns_ football. Nor soccer. Nor basketball. Really nobody owns any sport. Sports as sets of abstract rules for playing a game are not intellectual property. Sure the NFL logo is trademarked but the NFL can't stop you from playing football in your backyard. They can't even stop you from promoting the match and selling tickets (though maybe your local municipality has something to say about an unregulated large gathering).

Yet in the world of eSports each game is in fact owned as the intellectual property of its publisher. So legally you cannot host your own monetized match of League of Legends without Riot's consent. You actually can't even play the game without adhering to Riot's private Terms of Service. While it makes sense for Riot to want sole ownership over their game I do wonder **how much does private ownership of games hurt eSports as an industry?**

## Traditional Sports vs eSports

Let's assume the professional scene of traditional sports like Football is a Good Business™ (despite some [serious evidence contrary](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s7N-xXGgdE4)). They have immense viewership, ad revenue, and social impact so most people would agree that professional sporting is a legitimate industry. Why isn't professional eSporting the same? Is it becuase video games are still too niche? Are video game demographics not prime for advertising spend? Do eSports leagues have structural issues with their organization and ability to monetize? Some combination of the above? The problem of effectively monetizing eSports (and competition in general) is complex and I won't tackle it here but I do want to bring up an often overlooked point around game ownership.

## The patches will continue until morale improves

While the NFL controls the exact ruleset its games are played under, the fundamental aspects that define the game are static and replicable by any other leagues or individuals. However with eSports the game is definitively owned by the developers and as such _all_ forms of play are under their control. In the case of League of Legends, for example, from the premier professional league to amateur leagues all the way to a few friends playing over the weekend - Riot Games dictates the rules. So if Riot decides to update the game everybody must comply and the previous form of the game is gone forever.
